§ 02 — Six Things You Wouldn't Notice

Findings hidden in the fine print.

Finding No. 1 · The Ranking Reframe

By total revenue, Capitol Square wins.
By productivity, it isn't even close.

Capitol Square is open 257 days a year and earns $254 a day. Tenney Park is open just 52 weekend days — but each of them brings in $945. The downtown stand looks like the breadwinner. The riverside cart is the workhorse.

Finding No. 2 · Off-Peak Peak

The biggest month wasn't July.
It was September.

September pulled $22,940 — beating July ($18,821) and August ($20,452). Madison's mild back-to-school weather, plus the East High kiosk reopening, makes early autumn the secret high season. October ($19,983) outperformed June ($18,028).

Finding No. 3 · The Strawberry Effect

One new recipe became 27% of the menu in seven months.

Strawberry Lemonade launched May 1. Between then and year-end, it earned $37,142 — more than a quarter of every dollar that crossed the counter post-launch. It overtook Classic Small in qty and now sits #2 by revenue, behind only Classic Large. New SKUs rarely move that fast.

Finding No. 4 · Identity Crisis

This is a "lemonade" business — but the fourth-best seller is a cookie.

The Lemon Sugar Cookie sold 7,428 units for $18,570. That's more cookies than any single beverage SKU except the two Classics. Worth re-reading the company name.

Finding No. 5 · Sun & Rain

An 80°F day is worth 1.8 cool days. Rain costs 22%.

Days where the high topped 80°F averaged $682 in revenue; days under 60°F averaged $380. Heavy-rain days (≥ ¼") came in 22% below dry days. Linda's instinct to schedule extra hours on hot Saturdays is empirically the right call.

Finding No. 6 · Two Rushes

The day has two peaks — noon, and again at 3pm.

Lunch (12pm: $29,039) and the after-school window (3pm: $28,584) are virtually tied. The 2pm dip is real, not noise — it's the gap between the downtown lunch crowd and the East High bell.

About this report

The Lemonade Annual is generated from one season's worth of point-of-sale, scheduling, and weather data — 38,558 line items across 24,655 transactions, joined to four stands, six employees, and 365 days of Madison weather. The numbers are real to the workbook; the workbook is synthetic, built for an AI demonstration. Any resemblance to actual lemonade businesses is, like a Saturday in July, coincidental and pleasant.

Charts hand-drawn in SVG. Set in Fraunces and Newsreader, with data in IBM Plex Mono. Pour responsibly.
